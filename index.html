4-week progression plan

Progress gradually — only increase volume when form is perfect.

Week 1–2
Foundation
Learn all movements. Focus 100% on form. Use the exact sets/reps listed. Rest fully between sets. Your body is adapting — don't rush.
Week 3–4
Volume up
Add 1 extra set per exercise. Reduce rest by 15 seconds. Try to hit the top of each rep range. Form must stay strict.
Week 5–6
Intensity up
Progress push-ups to diamond or decline. Add jump squats. Introduce 10-sec pauses at the hardest part of each move.
Week 7–8
Power phase
Add explosive variants: clap push-ups, jump squats, speed burpees. Reduce rest to 30–40 sec.

Diet tips for fat loss + lean muscle

🔥 Calorie target

Maintenance ~2,400–2,600 kcal. Aim for 2,000–2,200 kcal/day → slow fat loss, muscle preserved.

🥚 Protein priority

130–160g/day. Eggs, paneer, dal, chicken, fish, tofu. Keeps you full and defends muscle.

⏰ Meal timing

  • Breakfast within 1h of waking
  • Pre-workout: banana or light carbs
  • Post-workout: protein + carbs (curd + fruit)
  • Last meal 2h before sleep

🥦 What to eat more

  • Leafy greens, whole grains, oats, legumes
  • Fruits, groundnuts, flaxseeds

🚫 Reduce

  • Refined carbs, sugary drinks, fried snacks, late meals

💧 Hydration

3–3.5L daily. Drink 500ml right after waking.

Beginner mistakes to avoid

🚫 Skipping warm-up

Cold muscles = lower performance + injury risk. 5min minimum.

🎯 Ego reps

20 sloppy push-ups < 10 perfect ones. Form > quantity.

⏱️ Not resting enough

Rest 45–90 sec between sets for quality.

📈 Progressing too fast

Tendons adapt slower — follow weekly phases.

⚠️ Ignoring pain

Joint pain = stop immediately. Muscle burn = good.

🔄 Inconsistency + overtraining

Consistent 80% effort beats sporadic 100%.

📸 Not tracking

Take photos & log reps. Motivation follows proof.
