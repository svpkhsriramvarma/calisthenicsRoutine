4-Week Progression Plan

Progress gradually — your body needs adaptation time. Only increase volume or difficulty when you can complete all sets with good form.

Week 1–2
Foundation
Learn all movements. Focus 100% on form. Use the exact sets/reps listed. Rest fully between sets. Your body is adapting — don't rush.
Week 3–4
Volume up
Add 1 extra set per exercise. Reduce rest by 15 seconds. Try to hit the top of each rep range. Form must stay strict.
Week 5–6
Intensity up
Progress push-ups to diamond or decline. Add jump squats to leg day. Introduce 10-sec pauses at the hardest part of each move.
Week 7–8
Power phase
Add explosive variants: clap push-ups, jump squats, speed burpees. Reduce rest to 30–40 sec. Add a 4th circuit on HIIT day.

Diet Tips for Fat Loss + Lean Muscle

Calorie target (approximate)

At 81 kg with moderate activity, your maintenance is ~2,200–2,400 kcal. To lose fat: eat 1,800–2,000 kcal/day. Never go below 1,700 kcal — you'll lose muscle and tank your energy. Weigh yourself weekly (same time, same conditions) and adjust if progress stalls for 2+ weeks.

Protein — your top priority

Aim for 120–140g of protein daily. This preserves muscle while you lose fat. Good sources: eggs, paneer, dal, curd, chicken, fish, tofu, rajma, sprouts. Include protein in every meal — not just dinner.

Meal timing

  • Eat within 1 hour of waking up — stops morning cortisol from eating muscle
  • Pre-workout: a banana or small carb snack 30–45 min before training
  • Post-workout: protein + carbs within 45 min (e.g., eggs + rice, or curd + fruit)
  • Last meal 2 hours before sleep

What to eat more of

  • Vegetables (fill half your plate) — especially leafy greens
  • Whole grains: brown rice, oats, whole wheat roti
  • Fruits: banana, apple, papaya, watermelon
  • Legumes: rajma, chana, moong dal
  • Healthy fats: groundnuts, flaxseeds, coconut in moderation

What to reduce

  • Refined carbs: maida, white bread, biscuits, packaged snacks
  • Sugary drinks: soda, juice, chai with excess sugar
  • Fried foods: samosa, pakora, puri
  • Late night heavy meals

Hydration

You're already drinking 6+ glasses — good start. Aim for 3–3.5 litres daily. Drink 500ml water first thing after waking. Staying hydrated reduces false hunger signals and improves fat metabolism.

Belly fat specifically

You cannot spot-reduce belly fat with exercises alone. Belly fat responds to overall fat loss (calorie deficit + exercise), reduced stress, and better sleep. Be patient — it's usually the last to go for stocky builds, but it will come off consistently.

Energy, Recovery & Posture

Sleep — your biggest lever

You're sleeping 5–6 hours — this is significantly limiting your fat loss, muscle gain, and energy. During sleep, your body releases growth hormone and repairs muscle tissue. Aim for minimum 7 hours. Even adding 45 minutes will make a noticeable difference within 2 weeks.

  • Set a consistent bedtime — even weekends
  • No screens 30 min before bed
  • Keep your room cool and dark
  • Avoid caffeine after 4 PM

Boosting inconsistent energy

  • Eat breakfast within 1 hour of waking — stabilizes blood sugar
  • Avoid large carb-heavy lunches (causes energy crash)
  • Take a 10–15 min walk after meals — improves alertness
  • 5–10 min sunlight exposure in the morning resets your circadian rhythm
  • Drink water consistently — even mild dehydration kills energy
  • If energy is very low, do your workout anyway at lower intensity — movement generates energy

Recovery between sessions

  • Muscle soreness (DOMS) on days 1–2 is normal — keep moving gently
  • Foam rolling or self-massage for sore muscles before bed
  • Cold water splash after workouts reduces inflammation
  • If severely sore, do a lighter version of that day's workout rather than skipping
  • Don't train a muscle group until soreness is below 4/10

Posture correction (for long sitting hours)

  • Every hour of sitting: stand up and do 10 shoulder rolls + 10 chest openers
  • Doorway stretch daily: 2 × 30 sec — opens tight chest from slouching
  • Chin tucks: 3 × 10 reps daily — combats forward head posture from screens
  • Cat-cow stretch: every morning, 10 reps — mobilizes the spine
  • Hip flexor stretch daily — sitting tightens hip flexors, causing lower back pain
  • Strengthen your glutes and back — weak posterior chain = bad posture
  • Consider a lumbar support cushion while sitting

Stress & cortisol

High stress = high cortisol = belly fat storage. Manage stress through 5 min of deep breathing daily, reducing doom-scrolling, and getting outside. Yoga-style workouts on Saturday help directly.

Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping warm-up

Cold muscles tear easily and perform worse. Never skip the 5-minute warm-up. It also mentally primes you for the session.

Ego reps — quantity over quality

Doing 20 sloppy push-ups is worse than 10 perfect ones. Bad form recruits the wrong muscles and causes injuries. Always prioritize form — drop the reps if you need to.

Not resting enough between sets

Rushing sets means you can't perform the next set with good form. Rest 45–90 seconds between sets as noted. It's not laziness — it's how you get stronger.

Progressing too fast

Your tendons and joints adapt slower than your muscles. Adding too much volume too soon causes overuse injuries. Stick to the 4-week phase plan and add difficulty gradually.

Ignoring pain

Muscle burn = good. Sharp or joint pain = stop immediately. Never push through joint pain. Rest it, ice it, and if it persists, see a doctor before continuing.

Inconsistency followed by overtraining

Missing a week then doing double sessions is a recipe for injury and burnout. Consistent moderate effort beats sporadic intense effort every time. Show up 6 days at 80% rather than 2 days at 100%.

Not tracking progress

Take a photo on Week 1, Week 4, Week 8. Log your reps each session. Numbers don't lie — seeing improvement is the #1 motivation tool.

Expecting fast results

Your body type (endomorph/stocky) loses fat more slowly but builds solid, dense muscle. Visible results typically appear in 6–10 weeks of consistent work. Trust the process.
